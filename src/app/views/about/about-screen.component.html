Motivation

Determining the quality of life is a subject that has gained increasing attention over time precisely because of its socio-economic implications. More precisely, based on this data, the political decision makers can develop methodologies and public policies aimed at the inclusion of disadvantaged people and the development of underdeveloped regions; the business environment can identify new opportunities; and civil society can monitor government activity in terms of public policies implemented to improve living standards.

eLIL (European Life Index Leaf) is a visual interface that allows users to easily observe and analyze the factors influencing the quality of life of the population in EU member states and the UK, which left the Eurozone in early 2020. The data used by eLIL is projected through eLIF (European Life Index Framework), a framework that automates the collection, processing, and aggregation of data analyzed both in the form of 8+1 dimensions and into a single global indicator, QoLI (Quality of Life Index).

QoLI is a methodology developed [1][2]out of the necessity to cover a much broader range of factors that significantly influence the quality of life compared to those included in other well-known indices such as GDP, HDI, or WHOQOL.
